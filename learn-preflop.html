Preflop Fundamentals

Opening ranges, 3-bet strategy, big blind defence, combo math, and preflop heuristics to build a solid foundation before the flop.

Position & Opening Ranges

Position is the single biggest lever in preflop decisions. The later you act post-flop, the wider you can profitably open. The table below summarises typical 6-max opening widths and their rationale.

Pos Chip Typical open % Key holdings Why so tight / wide?
UTG UTG ~14 % TT+, AJs+, KQs, AQo+ 5 players left to act; expect resistance. Premium/nutted hands only.
MP MP ~18 % + 99, ATs, KJs, QJs, AJo One less player vs UTG; can add strong broadways and suited Ax.
CO CO ~25 % + 77–88, suited connectors, KQo Only BTN/blinds left; position advantage grows, add speculative hands.
BTN BTN ~42 % + all suited Ax, T9s–54s, Axo down to A7o Always IP post-flop. Steal equity vs blinds. Widest profitable range.
SB SB ~35 % Similar to CO, tighter than BTN Always OOP vs BB; must fight for pot with a positional handicap.

3-Bet Strategy

A balanced 3-bet range has value hands we want to build a pot with and bluffs we're happy to fold to a 4-bet — ideally hands that block villain's nutted range.

Value tier

  • AA, KK, QQ, JJ
  • AKs, AKo
  • (IP: TT, AQs vs loose opens)

Bluff tier

  • A2s–A5s (IP/blind)
  • KQs, QJs (vs EP wide)
  • Suited connectors w/ blockers

Size

  • IP: ~2.5–3× open
  • OOP: ~3–3.5× open
  • vs BTN steal: can go 3.2× IP

Key concept A2–A5 suited are ideal 3-bet bluffs: they block AA/AK (villain's most likely 4-bet hands) and have reasonable equity when called.

BB Defence (vs Open)

The BB gets a discount to see flops. You must defend wide enough to prevent opponents from profitably stealing with any two cards.

Vs open fromMin defence %Notes
BTN 2.5×~44 %Wide — suited connectors, weak Ax, offsuit broadways
CO 2.5×~38 %Can tighten slightly; CO range is stronger
MP 2.5×~30 %MP opens tight; less need to defend 2-gappers
UTG 3×~25 %UTG range crushes; fold all non-connected rags

Reminder Being OOP post-flop costs equity. Calling with speculative hands (suited connectors, pairs) in BB is fine — but have a plan for common board textures.

Preflop Heuristics

📍

Position > cards (mostly)

A mediocre hand IP is often better than a marginal hand OOP. Fold J9o UTG, open it BTN.

🔢

Raise size should be consistent

Vary size by stack depth and player pool, not by hand strength. 2.5× most spots; 3× vs loose BB.

🃏

Suitedness > connectedness

A7s > J9o in almost every spot. Suited cards add ~3–4 % equity vs a random hand.

⚖️

Don't cold-call too wide

Cold-calling a 3-bet is often dominated. Have a clear 4-bet or fold plan with marginal hands.

🎯

Stack depth changes everything

At 40bb: shove/fold. At 100bb: normal ranges apply. At 200bb+: implied odds up, speculative hands gain value.

Preflop Study Checklist

  • Memorise UTG open range (≈ 14 %) cold
  • Practice BTN open from SB defence perspective — what do you 3-bet/call/fold?
  • Know your 3-bet value hands by position (EP vs LP open)
  • Add A2s–A5s as 3-bet bluffs IP — track results for 50 sessions
  • Review BB defend decisions in your last session HH
  • Quiz yourself on combo counts for common 3-bet ranges
  • Identify one spot per session where you called/folded incorrectly preflop

Drill Ideas

🎴 Range flashcard drill

Pick a random position + position opener. List your calling, 3-bet, and fold combos aloud before checking vs chart.

🔄 Combo counting warm-up

Before each session: pick 3 hands (e.g. KQs, 99, AJo) and state their combo counts and blockers from memory.

📹 HH spot review

Pull 3 preflop decisions from your last session. Mark each as correct / leak / marginal and note why.
