Postflop Foundations

C-bet concepts, board texture analysis, pot odds, draws, and equity references to build confident flop decisions.

C-Bet Concepts

A continuation bet (c-bet) maintains the initiative from preflop. Profitability depends on your range advantage, nut advantage, and the board texture relative to both ranges. Betting always without checking is exploitable.

High c-bet frequency

IP vs single caller on dry boards (A72r, K83r). You hit these boards harder as the preflop raiser.

  • Size: 25–33 % pot (dry, static)
  • Full range or near-full range bet viable
  • Villain can't construct strong defence range vs tiny bet

Selective c-bet

Dynamic, coordinated boards (T98dd, 876ss). Your range advantage shrinks and villain has many draws and strong holdings.

  • Size: 50–75 % pot (dynamic/wet)
  • Bet your strong hands + best draws; check back medium equity
  • Build pot with value; protect equity with semi-bluffs

OOP c-bet caution

Firing c-bets out of position is punishing. Prefer a checking strategy that includes strong hands to prevent exploitation.

  • Check-raise strong hands to deny free cards
  • Check-call medium strength / draws
  • Check-fold weak air / dominated pairs

Board Textures

Read the board before deciding on a bet/check. Key dimensions: dry vs wet (draws available?), high vs low (who connects?), paired vs unpaired.

A♠ 7♥ 2♦

Dry / Static

Minimal draws. PFR range crushes — bet small, frequently. Villain struggles to find calls with unpaired hands.

J♠ T♦ 9♣

Wet / Dynamic

Flush and straight draws everywhere. Bet larger or check back with medium equity. Protect strong hands.

K♣ 6♦ 2♠

High card / Paired low

King hits PFR range hard. Low side almost irrelevant. Excellent spot for high-frequency small c-bets IP.

8♦ 7♦ 6♠

Low / Connected

BB and lower position defenders connect here. PFR loses range advantage significantly. Often better to check and re-evaluate.

Q♥ Q♦ 4♣

Paired board

Trips are rare for both — focus on who holds the Q more often. PFR range contains more QQ/KQ/AQ. Prefer small size.

A♠ K♦ Q♣

Broadway / Monotone

Both ranges can contain straights. Monotone boards: villain's flush draw concern limits your thin value betting. Tread carefully.

Pot Odds Quick Reference

Pot odds tell you the minimum equity you need to profitably call. Formula: Call / (Pot + Call).

⅓ pot betNeed 25 % equity to call
½ pot betNeed 25 % equity to call
¾ pot betNeed 30 % equity to call
Pot-sized betNeed 33 % equity to call
2× pot overbetNeed 40 % equity to call

Note Implied odds can justify calling with less equity when stacks are deep and villain can pay off on future streets.

Draw Equity Reference

The rule of 2 & 4: multiply outs × 4 on the flop (2 streets) or × 2 on the turn (1 street) for approximate equity %.

Flush draw (9 outs)
~36 % flop · ~18 % turn
Open-ended straight (8 outs)
~32 % flop · ~16 % turn
Gutshot (4 outs)
~16 % flop · ~8 % turn
Two overcards (6 outs)
~24 % flop · ~12 % turn
FD + OESD (15 outs)
~54 % flop · ~30 % turn

Approximate — exact values depend on villain's cards. Rule of 2&4 is close enough for in-game use.

Postflop Heuristics

🎯

Ask: "Who does this board favour?"

Before betting, identify whether your range or villain's range connects harder. That determines bet frequency and size.

📐

Small bet = polarise or give up cheaply

A 25 % bet often serves as a "equity denial" bet with a strong merged range. A large bet says "I'm polarised."

🔄

Check to protect your checking range

If you always c-bet everything, your checks become transparent air. Checking strong hands balances your range.

💧

Draws semi-bluff, don't just call

Semi-betting draws puts villain to a decision, adds fold equity, and builds pot for value when you hit.

🃏

Top pair, top kicker ≠ always value

TPTK is a bluff-catcher on wet boards. Don't stack off vs a large bet on J-T-9 with AJ when villain bets pot.

Postflop Study Checklist

  • For each flop in a session, classify texture before acting
  • Track c-bet success rate — are you betting too wide on bad textures?
  • Review every instance you called a large bet: did you have the equity?
  • Practice the rule of 2&4 in real-time for 10 sessions
  • Tag HH spots where you missed a semi-bluff opportunity
  • Identify one board per session where you should have checked back
  • Study a "merged range" vs "polarised range" example in solver output
